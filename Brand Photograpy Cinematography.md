# Brand Photography & Cinematography Guidelines
## Visual Style Guide for the Chef's Atelier & Quiet Luxury Aesthetic

---

## 1. Core Architectural Concept
The physical space is designed to reflect a "chef’s atelier" merged seamlessly with the principles of quiet luxury. Rather than feeling like a sterile backdrop for a technology commercial, the environment mirrors a premium lifestyle documentary, contemporary architectural home, or high-end boutique wellness retreat. The kitchen environment serves as a localized, warm hub for clean eating and creative expression where advanced technology remains secondary, supportive, and deeply integrated into a high-quality residential setting.

### Material Palette & Textures
The materials selected evoke a grounded, premium lifestyle through tactile, high-quality finishes:
* **Countertops:** Designed as a thick, solid surface made of polished concrete or a matte engineered quartz, showcasing a subtle, fine-grained stone texture that acts as an artisanal canvas for meal preparation.
* **Cabinetry:** Flat-panel lower cabinetry featuring a natural wood veneer—specifically walnut or medium-stained oak—with a prominent vertical grain to ground the space in organic warmth.
* **Walls & Surrounds:** Background walls feature a smooth, non-reflective matte painted drywall or plaster finish in an off-white or light-gray tone.
* **Tactile Accents:** Structural finishes are balanced by the soft, premium textures of linen textiles and artisanal ceramic tiles.
* **Natural Elements:** Strategic integration of natural greenery and fresh herbs connects the environment back to raw, essential nutrition.

---

## 2. Lighting & Atmospheric Palette
The environmental lighting avoids the cold, blue, and hyper-exposed aesthetics typical of tech-showroom environments. Instead, it uses a highly intentional, serene lighting scheme:
* **Primary Illumination:** Bathed in natural golden hour daylight or soft, directional window light with a warm color temperature ranging between 2,000 K and 3,500 K.
* **Contrast & Depth:** Soft, diffused ambient fill creates gentle shadow transitions and warm highlights, providing a bright, airy, yet deeply lived-in aesthetic.
* **Strict Exclusions:** The environment strictly avoids cold blue lighting, clinical white environments, harsh top-down illumination, or strong fluorescent appearances.

---

## 3. Spatial Curation & Styling
The layout balances the efficiency required by a busy lifestyle with a mindful, relaxed sense of calm control:
* **Spatial Character:** Set within contemporary residential kitchens, premium apartments, or architectural homes that feel open, elegant, and elevated.
* **Decorative Accents:** The environment is thoughtfully accessorized using high-end, intentional items such as curated cookbooks, fruit bowls, ceramic vessels, and architectural objects.
* **Clutter Management:** While the space feels authentic and lived-in, it strictly avoids visual clutter to maintain a serene, balanced aesthetic where the mind can focus on wellness without friction.

---

## 4. Still Photography Guidelines
To capture the physical space in a manner that aligns with the premium, architectural aesthetic, all standalone imagery must adhere to these precise photographic standards:
* **Contextual Editorial Style:** Imagery must reflect contemporary interior architectural and editorial lifestyle photography. The composition should capture a bright, airy, and authentically lived-in aesthetic rather than a staged, sterile studio look.
* **Optics & Framing:** Standardize captures using a 24mm to 35mm tilt-shift lens on a full-frame sensor. This specific setup ensures straight architectural lines, minimizes edge distortion, and presents a natural, true-to-life perspective of the environment.
* **Color Palette & Lighting:** Photographs must utilize a muted and earthy color palette. Leverage natural golden hour daylight (~2,000 K–3,500 K) paired with a soft, diffused fill to cultivate a serene, warm, and inviting atmosphere.
* **Compositional Curation:** Thoughtfully include high-end decorative items within the frame to ground the environment. However, spacing must be meticulously managed to preserve a balanced, intentional look and avoid visual clutter.
* **Strict Enhancements & Prohibitions:**
  * *No Text:* Do not include any visible text, branding, or graphic overlays within the photographic frame.
  * *No Artificial Chills:* Absolutely avoid cold, clinical, or harsh artificial lighting that disrupts the mindful, quiet luxury vibe of the space.

## 5. Video Photography & Cinematography Guidelines

### Core Narrative
Every video should communicate one or more of the following themes:
* Wellness without friction
* Nutrition as a personalized experience
* Technology serving people rather than dominating the experience
* The transformation of routine meal preparation into an intentional ritual
* Calm control amidst a busy lifestyle
* *Note: The appliance is never the hero by itself. The true subject is the relationship between the individual and the nourishment they create.*

### Visual Atmosphere & Desired Emotional Response
The overall experience should resemble a premium lifestyle documentary, culinary editorial, or luxury hospitality brand film rather than a technology commercial. Viewers should feel:
* Calm & Grounded
* Inspired & Capable
* Optimistic & Supported

### Brand Energy References
* High-end culinary storytelling
* Boutique wellness retreats
* Chef ateliers
* Quiet luxury hospitality experiences
* Contemporary architectural lifestyle films

### Cinematographic Technical Specifications

#### 1. Lighting
* **Preferred:** Natural golden-hour daylight, soft directional window light, diffused ambient fill, gentle shadow transitions, warm highlights, and subtle contrast.
* **Color Temperature:** Primary range of 2,000K–3,500K with warm neutral whites, natural wood tones, and earth-inspired color rendering.
* **Avoid:** Cold blue lighting, clinical white environments, harsh top-down lighting, strong fluorescent appearance, overly dramatic contrast, and tech-showroom aesthetics.

#### 2. Camera Language & Movement
* **Style:** Camera movement should feel intentional and human. Movement should create a sense of discovery rather than excitement.
* **Preferred:** Slow push-ins, slow pull-backs, gentle slider moves, controlled handheld movement, floating gimbal movement, and subtle parallax motion.
* **Avoid:** Fast whip pans, aggressive tracking shots, excessive camera shake, hyperactive social-media editing, and extreme action-sports cinematography.

#### 3. Lens Selection
* **24–35mm:** Environmental storytelling and architectural context.
* **50mm:** Lifestyle moments.
* **85mm:** Intimate details and expressions.
* **Macro Lenses:** Dedicated to ingredients, textures, and close-up food preparation rituals.
* *Note: Visual compression should feel natural and immersive. Avoid ultra-wide distortion and exaggerated perspective.*

### Subject Matter & Styling

#### 1. Subject Matter & Recommended Scenes
* **Ingredient Moments:** Fresh ingredients being loaded, macro details of textures, hands arranging ingredients, and nutritional preparation rituals.
* **Lifestyle Moments:** Busy professional beginning their day, parent preparing family nutrition, athlete reviewing meal plans, or an individual enjoying a quiet meal.
* **Product Interaction:** Ingredient loading, recipe customization, personalized meal creation, and finished meal presentation. Interactions must appear intuitive and effortless.

#### 2. Human Presence
* People should appear confident, relaxed, purposeful, engaged, and authentic.
* Expressions should feel genuine rather than overly enthusiastic.
* **Diversity:** Natural, observational representation showing a range of busy professionals, athletes, parents, and culinary enthusiasts of different ages and backgrounds.

#### 3. Food Styling
* Meals should appear fresh, balanced, nutrient-dense, and artisanal.
* Food must feel elevated but achievable; it should never feel artificial, heavily processed, or excessively indulgent. The viewer should believe they could realistically create and enjoy the meal.

## 6. Post-Production Guidelines

### Editing & Pacing
* **Pacing:** Deliberate, relaxed, reflective, and confident. Allow moments to breathe.
* **Shot Duration:** Favor longer shots, letting actions complete naturally. Use edits motivated by story rather than speed.
* **Transitions:** Use straight cuts, gentle dissolves, and natural motion transitions. Avoid trend-based effects, flash transitions, excessive speed ramps, or distracting motion graphics.

### Color Grading
* **Desired Look:** Warm neutrals, natural skin tones, muted earth colors, soft contrast, and filmic highlight roll-off to create a premium, timeless, and tactile feel.
* **Avoid:** Over-saturation, teal-and-orange grading, high-HDR artificiality, extreme contrast, and futuristic neon palettes.

### Audio Direction
* **Preferred Sounds:** Ingredient handling, kitchen ambience, gentle mechanical operation, soft environmental sounds, and natural room tone.
* **Music:** Minimal, refined, contemporary, and emotional without becoming dramatic. Avoid aggressive electronic, industrial, or futuristic sound design.

---

## 7. Mission Communication Check (The Quality Filter)
Every finished video should answer **at least three** of the following questions with a "Yes" before approval:
1. Does the video make nutrition feel more accessible?
2. Does the video reduce perceived effort associated with meal preparation?
3. Does the video communicate personalization?
4. Does the video portray technology as supportive rather than dominant?
5. Does the video evoke calm confidence?
6. Does the video reinforce wellness and intentional living?
7. Does the video feel premium without feeling exclusive?

*If the asset answers "yes" to fewer than three questions, it must be revised before final approval.*
