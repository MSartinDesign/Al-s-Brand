# Brand Typography & Visual Design Guidelines: Alís

## 1. Introduction & Editorial Philosophy
The visual and typographic language of **Alís** is rooted in the concept of a **chef’s atelier**—a space where raw, essential nutrition is elevated into a personalized culinary art form [cite: 2, 19]. The typography must never feel cold, mechanical, or clinical [cite: 18]. Instead, it should project **quiet luxury**, advanced but supportive innovation, and absolute intentionality [cite: 16, 17, 18]. 

By balancing an elegant, high-contrast serif with a hyper-clean, modern geometric sans-serif, the brand embodies both artisanal mastery and seamless, effortless efficiency [cite: 13].

---

## 2. Primary Font: Playfair Display Medium
* **Purpose:** Headlines, Editorial Callouts, Hero Statements, and Section Markers.
* **Vibe:** Elegant, artisanal, elevated, traditional yet timeless.
* **Styling Rules:**
  * **Case:** **ALWAYS FULLY CAPITALIZED** for major brand expressions and structural headlines.
  * **Tracking / Letter-Spacing:** Expanded slightly (+5% to +10% or `0.06em` to `0.1em`) to allow the characters breathing room, reinforcing the premium, spacious luxury feel [cite: 17].
  * **Avoid:** Never use for long blocks of running text or small labels, as legibility decreases significantly.

---

## 3. Secondary Font: Inter
* **Purpose:** Subheadings, Body Narrative, Technical Data, UI Components, and Bulleted Specs.
* **Vibe:** Mindful, serene, clean, highly legible, modern, and efficient [cite: 13].
* **Styling Rules:**
  * **Case:** Sentence case or Title case. **NEVER** fully capitalized in body text.
  * **Weight Variants:** * *Inter Regular / Book:* For standard body copy and narrative descriptions.
    * *Inter Medium / Semi-Bold:* For subtitles, key metrics, and small labels.
  * **Line Height:** Generous line spacing (1.5× to 1.6×) to maintain a peaceful, structured, and easy-to-read layout that mimics a serene wellness ecosystem [cite: 13].

---

## 4. Typographic Hierarchy & Scale

| Element | Font Family | Case Style | Weight | Visual Scale / Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **H1: Hero Headline** | Playfair Display | **ALL CAPS** | Medium | Large, high-impact brand statements. Heavily letter-spaced. |
| **H2: Section Header** | Playfair Display | **ALL CAPS** | Medium | Introduces main content shifts (e.g., "MISSION", "VISION") [cite: 1, 9]. |
| **H3: Subheading** | Inter | Sentence/Title Case | Semi-Bold | Technical specifications, nutrient indicators, or chapter subtitles. |
| **Body Copy** | Inter | Sentence Case | Regular | Narratives, company background, and detailed consumer instructions [cite: 3]. |
| **Captions & Meta** | Inter | Sentence Case | Medium | Small text, instructional subtext, or subtle brand anchors. |

---

## 5. Core Rules for Layout & Implementation

### Rule 1: Embrace Open Space (The Serene Canvas)
White space is not empty space; it is a premium brand asset. Do not crowd the uppercase Playfair Display titles. Give headings generous top and bottom margins to mimic the minimal, high-end feel of a luxury resort or premium skincare line (e.g., Bulgari Hotels, Aesop) [cite: 17, 22, 24].

### Rule 2: Technology Must Support, Not Dominate
When displaying data, automated metrics, or nutritional ratios, use **Inter** to reflect high-tech engineering [cite: 7]. However, immediately frame or anchor that data with the warm, human, artistic character of **Playfair Display** to ensure the technology always feels secondary to the nutrition [cite: 18].

### Rule 3: Maintain High-Contrast Pairing
Never mix the two fonts inside a single running sentence. Use a strict linear separation: titles stand out as classic serifs, while descriptions and functional parameters remain perfectly geometric and legible.
